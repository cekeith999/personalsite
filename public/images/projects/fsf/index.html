Fashion Scholarship Fund (FSF) 2026

Merchandising Case Study — Winner

This project is my winning case study for the Fashion Scholarship Fund (FSF) 2026 Merchandising Competition, one of the most selective and prestigious pipelines into the fashion and retail industry. FSF partners directly with leading global brands and evaluates students on their ability to think strategically across consumer insight, merchandising, digital innovation, and business impact.

Project Overview

My submission was developed in response to the FSF 2026 Merchandising Brief, which challenges students to create a forward-looking, data-driven merchandising strategy grounded in real industry constraints and emerging consumer behavior. The case study was recognized for its depth of research, originality, and ability to connect technology, culture, and commerce into a cohesive retail vision.

About the Fashion Scholarship Fund Competition

The Fashion Scholarship Fund is a national nonprofit that supports and identifies top talent in fashion, retail, and business. Each year, FSF selects a small group of finalists across multiple tracks (Merchandising, Design, Marketing, Supply Chain) based on rigorous written case studies reviewed by executives from companies such as Nike, Adidas, Google, LVMH, and Estée Lauder.

Winning the FSF 2026 Merchandising Track signifies demonstrated excellence in:

  • Strategic merchandising and assortment planning
  • Consumer and market analysis
  • Financial literacy and retail KPIs
  • Omnichannel and digital retail thinking
  • Clear storytelling and executive-level communication

Case Study Focus

This case study explores how modern brands can merge performance, lifestyle, and technology to meet evolving consumer expectations around personalization, convenience, and cultural relevance.

The work centers on:

  • Translating macro consumer trends into actionable merchandising strategy
  • Designing assortments that balance innovation with commercial viability
  • Leveraging digital tools and AI-driven insights to enhance retail decision-making
  • Building a scalable, omnichannel ecosystem that connects product, platform, and experience

Rather than treating merchandising as purely operational, this project frames it as a creative and strategic discipline that shapes how consumers discover, value, and engage with products.

Research & Methodology

The case study is grounded in multi-layered research, including:

  • Industry reports and earnings transcripts
  • Consumer behavior and cultural trend analysis
  • Competitive landscape and brand positioning audits
  • Retail innovation and emerging technology research
  • Financial modeling, KPIs, and six-month rollout planning

Insights were synthesized into a clear merchandising narrative that connects consumer need → product strategy → distribution → experience → business outcomes.

Key Deliverables

  • Executive Summary and Strategic Thesis
  • Consumer Segmentation and Target Profiles
  • Competitive and Market Analysis
  • Product Assortment Strategy
  • Merchandise Planning and KPI Framework
  • Omnichannel Distribution and Retail Experience Strategy
  • Launch Roadmap and Future Growth Vision

Each section was designed to mirror how merchandising strategy is presented internally at global brands, prioritizing clarity, feasibility, and impact.

Outcome & Recognition

This project was selected as a winning submission for the FSF 2026 Merchandising Competition, earning recognition from industry judges and positioning me as a Fashion Scholarship Fund Scholar.

The case study reflects my ability to operate at the intersection of fashion, technology, and business strategy, and represents the foundation of my approach to modern merchandising: analytical, culturally fluent, and future-facing.

Skills Demonstrated

  • Merchandising Strategy & Assortment Architecture
  • Consumer Insight & Trend Translation
  • Retail Financial Planning & KPIs
  • Omnichannel & Digital Commerce Strategy
  • Strategic Storytelling & Executive Presentation

This project was completed independently as part of the Fashion Scholarship Fund 2026 national competition.

Some of my thoughts recently: People wear clothes like armor, not because they're cold but because they're scared of being seen for what they are, unfinished. Every thread says something about how far we've drifted from our own expression. Fashion started as protection, then communication, now it's performance. We forgot what we were trying to say. People talk about wanting authenticity, but no one really wants to be seen mid-process. We like the illusion of rawness, not the mess it takes to get there.
Everything about life contradicts itself. We chase freedom through structure. We find identity in repetition. We call our screens a window to the world while the world keeps shrinking behind them. People want to escape systems but still need something to belong to. That's the joke, everything that frees us also traps us. We hate the artificial but live through it anyway. It's not fake, it's just another layer of survival. Pretending might be the only thing that keeps us human.
Every invention feels like an echo. We build machines to talk for us, then forget what silence sounds like. We automate feeling, and it leaves us starving for the real thing. We keep trying to design empathy into the system, but maybe the system was never supposed to care. It's all theater, but that doesn't make it meaningless. It's still a stage where we can rehearse being alive, over and over, until it feels right again.
I work inside that space, between art and survival, between code and soul. I'm not trying to fix the contradiction. I'm trying to live in it. The goal isn't harmony, it's awareness. To notice the glitch and still make something from it. To build a world that doesn't exist yet and remind myself that creation is the only honest way to lie. Every mask, every prototype, every render is proof that I'm still learning how to translate being into form.
